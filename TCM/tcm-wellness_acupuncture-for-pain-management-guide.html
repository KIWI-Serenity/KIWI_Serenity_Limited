What Is Acupuncture?

Acupuncture is a key component of Traditional Chinese Medicine that involves inserting thin, sterile needles into specific points on the body. These points, called acupoints, are believed to stimulate the body's energy flow (Qi) and restore balance. Modern research suggests acupuncture works by releasing endorphins, improving blood circulation, and modulating the nervous system.

What to Expect During Your First Acupuncture Session

If you've never tried acupuncture, here's what typically happens:

1. Initial Consultation (30-60 minutes)

The practitioner will ask about your medical history, pain patterns, lifestyle, and diet. In TCM, they'll also examine your tongue and feel your pulse — these provide diagnostic clues about internal imbalances.

2. Treatment Session (30-45 minutes)

You'll lie comfortably on a treatment table. The practitioner inserts very thin, sterile needles at specific points. Most patients feel minimal sensation — perhaps a mild tingling or dull ache. Needles remain in place for 20-30 minutes while you relax.

3. Post-Treatment

Many patients feel deeply relaxed after acupuncture. Some experience immediate pain relief, while others notice gradual improvement over several sessions.

4. Treatment Course

For chronic pain, practitioners typically recommend 6-12 sessions, often starting with 2-3 sessions per week, then reducing frequency as improvement occurs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does acupuncture hurt?

Most patients feel minimal discomfort. Acupuncture needles are much thinner than hypodermic needles. You might feel a mild tingling, warmth, or dull ache — these are considered positive signs that Qi is moving.

How many sessions will I need?

For chronic pain, practitioners often recommend 6-12 sessions. Acute conditions may improve faster. Your practitioner will create a personalized treatment plan.

Is acupuncture safe?

Yes, when performed by qualified practitioners using sterile, single-use needles. Complications are extremely rare.

Can I combine acupuncture with my current medications?

Generally yes, but inform both your TCM practitioner and Western doctor about all treatments you're receiving.
