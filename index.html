Frequently Asked Questions from International Patients

Is medical travel to China safe?

Yes, when arranged through a verified coordinator and a licensed clinic. Reputable private clinics in Chengdu use internationally recognized equipment and sterilization protocols. Before committing, confirm the clinic's licensing, the treating doctor's credentials, and get a documented treatment plan and cost breakdown.

How much can I save on dental implants in Chengdu?

A single dental implant starts from $800 in Chengdu, compared to $4,000+ in the US or NZ — a potential saving of roughly 65%. Exact cost depends on implant brand and case complexity, confirmed after a clinic evaluation.

How do I verify a medical travel coordinator in China is legitimate?

Check for a registered business entity with a publicly verifiable number, a fee structure where medical costs are paid directly to the clinic (not the coordinator), and documented real patient cases with verifiable cost data. See our full Trust & Verification page for a complete checklist.

Do I need a special visa for medical or dental treatment in China?

No. A standard tourist visa is generally sufficient for short-term dental, TCM, or health screening trips. No long-term medical visa is required for most procedures.
