1. Introduction

KIWI Serenity Limited ("we", "us", or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy and personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect your personal information in accordance with New Zealand law, including the Privacy Act 2020.

This policy applies to all personal information we collect through our services, including but not limited to cross-border travel arrangements and non-medical logistical coordination relating to third-party services.

2. Information We Collect

We may collect the following types of personal information:

  • Identity Information: Name, passport/ID number, date of birth, nationality
  • Contact Information: Email address, phone number, residential address
  • Travel Information: Itinerary details, travel preferences, accommodation requirements
  • Health Information: Health-related information voluntarily provided by you for non-medical coordination and scheduling purposes only, which will only be collected if you expressly and voluntarily provide such information through our health questionnaire with explicit consent.
  • Financial Information: Payment details, billing address (processed securely through third-party providers)
  • Technical Information: IP address, browser type, device information, cookies data

3. How We Use Your Information

We use your personal information for the following purposes:

  • To provide and manage our cross-border tourism services
  • To coordinate travel and medical appointment arrangements as requested
  • To communicate with you about your itinerary, bookings, and updates
  • To process payments and maintain financial records
  • To comply with legal obligations under New Zealand law
  • To improve our services and customer experience
  • To ensure the security of our services and prevent fraud

Important Note Regarding Medical Information

Any health or medical information collected is used solely for coordination purposes to facilitate scheduling and communication with independent third-party service providers in China, at your request and with your consent. We do not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. All medical decisions and treatments are between you and the healthcare provider.

4. Disclosure of Your Information

We may disclose your personal information in the following circumstances:

  • To travel service providers (airlines, hotels, transportation companies) to fulfill your bookings
  • To independent medical or wellness service providers in China, solely for appointment scheduling and logistical coordination, where you have expressly requested such assistance.
  • To government authorities when required by New Zealand law
  • To our professional advisors (lawyers, accountants) as necessary
  • In connection with a business transaction, such as a merger or acquisition

We will never sell your personal information to third parties for marketing purposes.

5. International Transfers

As we provide cross-border tourism services, your personal information may be transferred to and processed in countries outside New Zealand. We ensure that such transfers comply with New Zealand privacy laws and that appropriate safeguards are in place to protect your information. You acknowledge that privacy laws in other jurisdictions may not provide the same level of protection as New Zealand law, and you consent to such transfers for the purposes described in this policy.

6. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal information against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. These measures include encryption, access controls, and secure data storage solutions.

7. Your Rights Under New Zealand Law

Under the Privacy Act 2020, you have the right to:

  • Access your personal information held by us
  • Request correction of any inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Withdraw consent for processing where consent is the legal basis
  • Make a complaint to the New Zealand Privacy Commissioner

To exercise these rights, please contact us using the details provided in this policy.

8. Retention of Information

We retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, including for legal, accounting, or reporting requirements. Typically, we retain client information for a period of 7 years after the completion of services, in accordance with New Zealand business record-keeping requirements.

9. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Our website may use cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your browsing experience and collect information about how you use our site. You can control cookie settings through your browser preferences.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The most current version will always be posted on our website with the effective date. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
