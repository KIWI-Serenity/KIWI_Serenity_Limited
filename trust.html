Is Medical Travel to China Safe?

Yes, when you work with a licensed clinic and a verified coordinator. Major cities such as Chengdu have private clinics and hospitals accustomed to treating international patients, using internationally recognized equipment and materials. The real risk in medical travel isn't the country — it's working with an unverifiable intermediary. Patients should confirm three things before committing to any coordinator: business registration, direct-payment fee structure, and documented real cases.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens if something goes wrong during treatment in China?

Reputable clinics provide full documentation of any procedure, including implant certificates and treatment records, which can be shared with your home doctor for follow-up care. Before committing, ask your coordinator directly how complications are handled and get this in writing.

Why is healthcare so much cheaper in China than in the US or NZ?

The primary drivers are lower clinical labor and facility overhead costs, and a large domestic manufacturing base for medical materials and equipment — not lower quality. Many clinics use the same internationally certified implant systems and imaging technology found in Western clinics.

Is medical tourism to China a scam?

Legitimate medical travel to China is not a scam, but the industry does have unverified intermediaries, as with any cross-border medical travel destination. The way to protect yourself is the verification checklist above: confirm registration, insist on direct clinic payment, and ask for real documented cases before you travel.
